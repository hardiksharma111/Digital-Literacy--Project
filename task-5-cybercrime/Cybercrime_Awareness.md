The Case Study: Phishing Attacks in Universities 

"Phishing remains one of the most prevalent cyber threats targeting students globally. In a typical scenario, a student receives an email that appears to be from their University’s IT department or a common service provider like Microsoft 365. The email often contains an 'urgent' message—such as a notice that their account will be deactivated unless they click a link to verify their credentials.

Once the student clicks the link, they are directed to a spoofed login page that looks identical to the official portal. Unknowingly, the student enters their username and password, which are immediately captured by the attacker. This can lead to identity theft, unauthorized access to academic records, and the potential for the attacker to send further phishing emails from the student’s official account. This case highlights that human psychology—specifically the sense of urgency—is often the weakest link in cybersecurity. Understanding these tactics is the first step toward building a resilient digital defense."

The Prevention Checklist (8 Tips)
Verify the Sender: Always check the actual email address, not just the display name.
Hover Before Clicking: Hover your mouse over any link to see the actual URL destination.
Use 2FA: Enable Two-Factor Authentication on all academic and social accounts.
Check for HTTPS: Ensure websites have a secure padlock icon in the browser bar.
Beware of Urgency: Be suspicious of emails demanding "immediate action."
Update Software: Keep your browser and OS updated to patch security holes.
Never Share OTPs: No official institution will ever ask for your One-Time Password.
Report Suspicious Activity: Use your university’s official channels to report phishing attempts.
