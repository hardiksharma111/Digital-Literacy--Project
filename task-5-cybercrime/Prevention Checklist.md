8 Tips for Digital Safety & Prevention

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Always add an extra layer of security (like an OTP or Authenticator App) to your VIT student portals and email accounts.
Verify the Sender's Domain: Scrutinize email addresses. A message from admin@vitbhopal.ac.in is official, but admin@vit-support-login.com is a scam.
The "Hover" Rule: Before clicking any link, hover your mouse over it to see the actual URL in the bottom corner of your browser.
Practice Password Hygiene: Use a unique, complex password for every platform. Avoid using your name, birthday, or "123456."
Check for HTTPS: Only enter personal data on websites that show the "padlock" icon and start with https://.
Secure Your Hardware: Never leave your laptop or phone unlocked in public spaces (like the library or canteen).
Limit Public Wi-Fi Use: Avoid accessing bank accounts or sensitive portals on open, public Wi-Fi without a VPN.
Report & Block: If you receive a suspicious message, report it to the National Cyber Crime Portal (1930) or your university IT cell immediately.
